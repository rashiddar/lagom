publications | منشورات

Below, you'll find a list of articles I've either written or co-authored with others for outside publications, starting with the most recent.

  • November 17th, 2017, "The Rise of the 'Westernists,'" The American Interest. Co-written with Shadi Hamid.

    • This piece pissed some people off, but I had a lot of fun drafting it.

  • March 30th, 2017, "Trump has vowed to eradicate ‘radical Islamic terrorism.’ But what about ‘Islamism’?," The Washington Post. Co-written with Shadi Hamid.

    • This was intended as an appetizer to get people interested in a longer report that I helped on alongside Shadi and Will McCants.

  • January 1st, 2017, "Islamism after the Arab Spring: Between the Islamic State and the nation-state," The Brookings Institution. Co-authored with Shadi Hamid and Will McCants.

    • This was a longer, academic report put out by our department on possible roads ahead for Islamism. I contributed most to the chapter titled, "ISIS and Islamists."

  • November 22nd, 2016, "Why it could be incredibly difficult to be Muslim and political in the age of Donald Trump," The Washington Post.

    • Published right as the Trump regime was beginning to take form.

  • July 21st, 2016, "Debating Muslim Futures," Convivencia Magazine.

    • Written in the wake of the Pulse shooting for a indie British Muslim mag. I think the debate is still ongoing internally within the American Muslim community…

  • July 15th, 2016, "Islamism, Salafism, and jihadism: A primer," The Brookings Institution. Co-written with Shadi Hamid.

    • This was perhaps our most “viral” piece. This surprised us, considering it had been nearly fifteen years after 9/11 when we wrote it—you would think there’d be more clarity on issues like these since then, no? Appears not.

  • April 21st, 2015. "The Other C-Word: Caliphate," Cicero Magazine.

    • I wrote this piece in grad school as the hysteria over ISIS began to take hold. I deeply regret that infantile headline.
